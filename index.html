Education

My education consisted of a major in Physics with minors in Mathematics and Computer Science. I graduated in 2012 with departmental honors from the Department of Physics and Astronomy.

I wanted to make myself as well rounded as possible, so I took a plethora of courses (both required and elective) in all three fields, and I also tutored Math from Algebra 1, through Calc 1-3, Differential Equations, and Lineary Algebra.

I was also a member of the Society of Physics Students for my entire tenure at Pitt. We took trips to the Allegheny Observatory, and met frequently to discuss happenings in the world of Physics and our current courseload.

My coursework was very intensive, requiring over 70 credits in Math and Physics, plus an additional 9 credits of Math and 12 credits of Computer Science for those respective minors.

The University of Pittsburgh was an awesome place to learn, and if I had a chance to do it all over again, I would certainly stick with Pitt as my college of choice.
